Production Handbook
Part 3 — Complete Content & Messaging Specification

Document Type: Content Implementation Specification

Status: Design Freeze Approved

Authority: This document defines the messaging, copy hierarchy, writing standards, trust framework, and conversion language for every page of the IVY Remodeling website.

Purpose

This document establishes how the website communicates with visitors.

It is not intended to provide marketing ideas or alternate copy.

It defines the approved messaging strategy and the rules that govern all written content throughout the website.

The implementation team shall not rewrite or reinterpret the approved messaging.

Core Brand Message

Everything on the website should reinforce one central idea:

Quality craftsmanship delivered honestly by a local contractor who genuinely cares about every project.

Every section should support this message.

If a paragraph, image, or feature does not reinforce this message, it should be reconsidered.

Brand Voice

The website should sound like a skilled craftsman speaking directly to a homeowner—not a salesperson.

Tone
Professional
Honest
Warm
Respectful
Confident
Helpful

Avoid
Pushy sales language
Excessive superlatives ("best", "number one", etc.)
Industry jargon without explanation
Fear-based marketing
Corporate buzzwords

Visitors should feel they are talking with a trustworthy contractor, not reading an advertisement.

Writing Principles

Every piece of copy should follow these principles:

1. Clarity First

Explain services in plain language.

Avoid unnecessary technical terminology.

2. Benefits Before Features

Instead of:

We use pressure-treated lumber.

Explain:

We build decks designed to withstand Arizona's changing weather and provide years of reliable use.

3. Customer-Focused

Write about the customer's goals before discussing the company.

Answer questions such as:

What problem does this solve?
Why should I care?
How will this improve my home?
4. Build Trust Gradually

Do not rely on a single "trust section."

Trust should accumulate throughout the website using:

Photography
Reviews
Guarantees
Experience
Licensing
Consistent messaging
5. Encourage Action Naturally

Calls-to-action should feel like the next logical step, not a sales pitch.

Preferred wording:

Request Your Free Estimate
Let's Talk About Your Project
Ready to Improve Your Home?
Start Your Estimate Today

Avoid aggressive CTAs such as:

BUY NOW
LIMITED TIME
DON'T MISS OUT
Homepage Messaging

The homepage must answer four questions in order:

1. Who are you?

IVY Remodeling is a local, owner-operated remodeling company serving Arizona's White Mountains.

2. What do you do?

High-quality remodeling, decks, carpentry, painting, electrical work, handyman services, and custom home improvements.

3. Why should I trust you?
Licensed
Insured
Veteran-Owned
10+ Years of Industry Experience
One-Year Satisfaction Guarantee
Personalized Service
Quality Craftsmanship
4. What should I do next?

Request a free estimate or call directly.

Every homepage section supports one of these questions.

Service Descriptions

Service descriptions should emphasize outcomes rather than tasks.

For example:

Instead of:

We build decks.

Use:

Create an outdoor space where your family can relax, entertain, and enjoy Arizona's beautiful seasons.

The emotional benefit should accompany the technical description.

About Page Messaging

The About page exists to build trust.

It should answer:

Who is Eli?
Why did he start IVY Remodeling?
What values guide the business?
What makes his approach different?

Avoid long autobiographies.

Focus on the customer's confidence.

Why Choose Us

This section should reinforce the company's strongest differentiators:

Owner-operated
Veteran-Owned
Licensed & Insured
Honest Communication
Detail-Oriented Craftsmanship
Local Knowledge
Satisfaction Guarantee

Each point should explain why it benefits the customer.

Gallery Messaging

The gallery should function as proof rather than decoration.

Every project should include:

Project title
Service type
Brief description
Optional location
Before/After comparison when available

Avoid generic captions such as "Project 1."

Testimonials & Reviews

Reviews must be authentic.

Until enough customer reviews exist:

Use clearly marked placeholders during development only.
Replace placeholders before production where possible.

Do not fabricate customer names or experiences.

FAQ Strategy

FAQs should address real concerns.

Examples include:

How long does a project take?
Do you offer free estimates?
Are you licensed and insured?
What areas do you serve?
What types of materials do you use?

Answers should be concise and reassuring.

Contact Page Messaging

The Contact page should reduce friction.

Communicate:

Friendly invitation to reach out.
Multiple contact methods.
Clear expectations for response time.
Simple estimate request process.

The page should end with reassurance rather than urgency.

Call-to-Action Rules

CTAs should be consistent across the entire site.

Primary:

Request Your Free Estimate

Secondary:

Call Now

Supporting:

View Our Work
Learn More
Explore Our Services

Do not introduce alternate CTA wording.

Consistency improves recognition and conversion.

Trust Framework

Every page should include at least two trust elements.

Examples:

Review snippet
Licensing badge
Guarantee
Experience statement
Project photography
Before/After comparison

Trust should never depend on one dedicated page.

Placeholder Content Policy

Approved placeholders include:

Stock project photography
Stock owner portrait
Placeholder review cards
Temporary gallery images

All placeholders must:

Match the brand style.
Be easy to replace.
Never require layout changes.

Every placeholder should be documented for future replacement.

Content Consistency Rules

The implementation team shall ensure:

Service names remain identical throughout the site.
CTA wording is consistent.
Headings follow a logical hierarchy.
Tone remains consistent.
No contradictory messaging appears between pages.
