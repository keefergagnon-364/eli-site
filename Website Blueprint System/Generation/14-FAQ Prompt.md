# 14 - FAQ Prompt

## Purpose

Generate the FAQ Section for the website.

The FAQ Section should:

* Improve SEO
* Answer customer questions
* Build trust
* Handle objections
* Reduce quote friction

The FAQ section is not simply a list of questions.

It should help visitors feel confident about contacting the business while supporting search engine visibility.

Ranked in order of importance:

1. Improve SEO
2. Answer Customer Questions
3. Build Trust
4. Handle Objections
5. Reduce Quote Friction

1. Discovery Questionnaire
2. Website Strategy
3. Customer Questions
4. Google Searches
5. Competitor Websites
6. Google Business Profile Reviews
7. Testimonials
8. Owner Experience

Prioritize real customer questions whenever available.

# FAQ Content Rules

Answers should:

* Be clear
* Be helpful
* Be concise
* Build trust

Preferred length:

2-4 sentences.

Avoid:

* Long paragraphs
* Technical jargon
* Marketing buzzwords
* Overly promotional language

---

# Trust Rules

When possible use FAQs to reinforce:

* Experience
* Professionalism
* Licensing
* Insurance
* Guarantees
* Process Transparency

Only use information supported by project documents.

Never invent qualifications or policies.

---

# SEO Rules

When appropriate:

* Include service keywords naturally
* Include location keywords naturally
* Support local search intent

Do not keyword stuff.

SEO should never reduce readability.

---

# Objection Handling Rules

Address common concerns such as:

* Pricing Questions
* Timeline Questions
* Service Area Questions
* Licensing Questions
* Insurance Questions
* Quality Concerns
* Process Questions

Focus on reducing uncertainty.

---

# FAQ Quantity Guidelines

Target:

8-13 Frequently Asked Questions

Generate additional questions only when they provide significant value.

Avoid filler content.

---

# Failure Conditions

The FAQ Section fails review if:

* SEO opportunities are ignored
* Customer questions are not answered
* Trust opportunities are ignored
* Objections are not addressed
* Excessive text is present
* Generic filler questions are included
* Keyword stuffing occurs

Revise until all failure conditions are resolved.
